# Eiffel Tower (Tour Eiffel) - Detailed Visitor Research

**Sources:** Official website toureiffel.paris (ticket prices, opening hours, key figures), Wikipedia (floor details, facilities), Wikimedia Commons (images)

---

## 1. What to See on Each Level

### Ground Level / Esplanade (地面层)
- **Height:** 0m (ground)
- **What's here:** The four massive iron pillars (each 25m wide at the base) form the entrance. Each pillar houses stairs and elevators to the upper floors. The esplanade area under the tower is free to access.
- **Features:** Ticket offices, security checkpoints, gift shops at the base of each pillar, information boards, and the bust of Gustave Eiffel. Educational panels about the tower's construction are displayed on the pillars.
- **For elderly:** The esplanade itself is flat and easy to walk. Looking up through the iron lattice from directly below is an impressive experience even without going up. Benches available in the surrounding Champ de Mars gardens.

### 1st Floor / Premier Etage (第一层)
- **Height:** 57m (187 feet)
- **Area:** 4,200 sq meters (the largest platform)
- **What's here:**
  - **Glass floor panels** - transparent sections allowing you to look down to the ground (thrilling but safe)
  - **"Madame Brasserie" restaurant** (formerly Le 58 Tour Eiffel) - casual dining with Paris views
  - **The Vertigo experience** - a walkable bridge/glass walkway attraction (free for ticket holders, recently reopened)
  - **Historical exhibition** about the tower's construction
  - **Gift shop and snack bars**
  - **Toilets** available on this level
- **For elderly:** HIGHLY RECOMMENDED. This is the most spacious and comfortable level. The glass floor is exciting but completely safe. The restaurant allows for a seated rest. The 1st floor has the most accessible facilities and the most space to walk around without feeling cramped. Views of Paris landmarks are excellent from this height - you can clearly see the Seine, Trocadero, and Champ de Mars.

### 2nd Floor / Deuxieme Etage (第二层)
- **Height:** 116m (377 feet)
- **Area:** 1,430 sq meters
- **What's here:**
  - **Le Jules Verne** - Michelin-starred gourmet restaurant (managed by chef Frederic Anton since 2019; has its own private elevator from the south pillar)
  - **Panoramic viewing areas** with orientation panels showing which landmarks are visible in each direction
  - **Champagne kiosk** (can be purchased as add-on with ticket)
  - **Gift shop and snack bar**
  - **Viewing telescopes**
- **For elderly:** Excellent panoramic views. This is where the view starts to become truly spectacular - you can see the full layout of Paris. The platform is smaller than the 1st floor but still comfortable. Strong winds can be felt at this height, so bring a light jacket even in spring.

### Summit / Top / Sommet (顶层)
- **Height:** 276m (906 feet) for the public viewing area
- **Area:** 250 sq meters (very small)
- **What's here:**
  - **Gustave Eiffel's restored office/apartment** - with lifelike mannequins of Eiffel and Thomas Edison, period furniture
  - **Champagne bar** at the very top
  - **360-degree panoramic views** - on a clear day you can see up to 70km
  - **Orientation maps** identifying landmarks in every direction
- **For elderly:** The summit is reached by a separate smaller elevator from the 2nd floor. The space is quite compact and can feel crowded during peak times. It is very windy and noticeably colder (the official site warns to "wrap up"). The summit is NOT accessible to people with reduced mobility. For elderly visitors who are in reasonable health, it is worth the trip for the unique experience, but those who are uncomfortable with heights or tight spaces may prefer to stay on the 2nd floor.

## 2. Best Viewpoints and Photo Spots

### Best spots to photograph THE tower (from outside):

1. **Trocadero / Palais de Chaillot (特罗卡德罗广场)** - THE classic photo spot
   - Located directly across the Seine, northwest of the tower
   - Provides the iconic front-facing symmetrical view with the tower perfectly centered
   - Best for: sunrise and golden hour shots
   - Metro: Trocadero (Lines 6, 9)

2. **Champ de Mars (战神广场)** - The grand lawn southeast of the tower
   - The long green lawn provides a beautiful foreground
   - Great for photos with the tower as backdrop
   - Best for: afternoon/evening, picnic photos
   - Note: can be very crowded in peak season

3. **Pont d'Iena (耶拿桥)** - The bridge directly connecting Trocadero to the tower
   - Excellent for closer symmetrical shots
   - Good for photos with the Seine in the foreground

4. **Pont de Bir-Hakeim (比尔哈凯姆桥)** - Southeast of the tower
   - Famous two-level bridge with elegant columns
   - Provides a cinematic, slightly angled view with the bridge columns framing the tower
   - Popular movie filming location

5. **Rue de l'Universite** - A residential street northeast of the tower
   - Classic "Eiffel Tower peeking over Parisian buildings" shot
   - A quintessential Paris street-scene photo

### Best views FROM the tower:

- **1st Floor:** Best for seeing the Champ de Mars, Trocadero fountains, and the Seine in detail. Buildings and people are still recognizable.
- **2nd Floor:** The sweet spot for panoramic photography. You can see the Arc de Triomphe, Sacre-Coeur, Notre-Dame, Les Invalides, and Montparnasse Tower clearly. Wide enough for good photos but high enough for a panoramic feel.
- **Summit:** The most dramatic views. On clear days you can identify landmarks in every direction. However, photos can look somewhat flat due to the extreme height.

## 3. Recommended Visit Approach for Elderly (老年人游览建议)

### Elevator vs. Stairs:

| Option | Details | Elderly Recommendation |
|--------|---------|----------------------|
| **Elevator to 2nd floor** | Adult: 23.50 EUR | RECOMMENDED - most comfortable option |
| **Elevator to Summit** | Adult: 36.70 EUR | Recommended if in good health |
| **Stairs to 2nd floor** | Adult: 14.80 EUR (674 steps) | NOT recommended for elderly |
| **Stairs + Elevator to Summit** | Adult: 28.00 EUR | NOT recommended for elderly |

**Strong recommendation for elderly visitors: Take the elevator both up and down.** The stairs involve 674 steps to the 2nd floor (equivalent to climbing a 40-story building). This is physically demanding even for fit adults.

### Suggested Visit Plan for Elderly:

1. **Buy tickets online in advance** (at www.toureiffel.paris) - this avoids the ground-level queue which can exceed 1-2 hours in peak season
2. **Take elevator to 2nd floor first** - enjoy the panoramic views, take photos
3. **Optional: Take the second elevator to the Summit** - only if weather is clear and the visitors are comfortable. Skip if it's windy, cold, or crowded
4. **Come back down to 1st floor** - walk around, see the glass floor, visit the exhibition
5. **Have a meal or drink** at Madame Brasserie (1st floor) or grab something at a snack bar
6. **Rest on the 1st floor** before descending - there are seating areas

### Important Notes for Elderly:
- **The summit and stairways are NOT suitable for people with reduced mobility** (stated on the official website)
- Disabled visitors (with disability card) pay reduced rates: 6.00 EUR to 2nd floor, 9.20 EUR to summit
- There is NO left luggage facility - travel light
- Bring a warm layer - it is always windy on the upper levels, even in spring
- Large luggage and pets are not allowed
- Security bag checks are required at entry

## 4. Best Time of Day for Visiting and Photography

### For visiting the tower (going up):

- **Best: Early morning (9:30 AM opening) or evening (after 7 PM)**
  - Morning: shortest queues, clear light for views, less crowded platforms
  - Evening: magical experience with the city lights, the tower sparkles every hour on the hour for 5 minutes after dark
- **Avoid: 11:00 AM - 4:00 PM** - peak crowding, longest queues
- **Spring (late March/April) specific:** Opening hours are 9:30 - 23:00. Last ascent at 10:45 PM. Average temperature around 9-12 degrees C. The tower lights up at sunset (roughly 8:00 PM in late March/early April).

### For photographing the tower from outside:

- **Sunrise (Trocadero):** 7:00-8:00 AM in early April - golden light, almost no crowds, stunning photos
- **Golden hour/Sunset:** The tower faces roughly NW-SE, so sunset light (from the west) beautifully illuminates the tower when viewed from Trocadero
- **Blue hour (just after sunset):** 8:30-9:00 PM in early April - the tower is lit up against a deep blue sky. This is considered the most photogenic time.
- **Hourly sparkle:** Every hour on the hour after dark, the tower sparkles with 20,000 lights for 5 minutes. Last sparkle at 11:00 PM (in spring).

### For your trip dates (March 31 - April 2):
- Sunrise: approximately 7:30 AM
- Sunset: approximately 8:15 PM
- Opening hours: 9:30 - 23:00
- Weather: expect 8-15 degrees C, bring layers

## 5. Suggested Duration

Based on the official site information and mainstream travel advice:

| Visit Type | Duration | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| **Quick visit (2nd floor only, elevator)** | 1.5 - 2 hours | Including queues with pre-booked tickets |
| **Standard visit (Summit + both floors, elevator)** | 2 - 2.5 hours | Recommended for most visitors |
| **Extended visit (all floors + meal)** | 3 - 3.5 hours | If dining at Madame Brasserie |
| **Outside photography only (Trocadero + Champ de Mars)** | 30 - 60 min | Free, no tickets needed |

### Recommended for elderly parents: 2.5 - 3 hours total
- Allow 30 min for security + elevator wait (even with pre-booked tickets)
- 20-30 min on 2nd floor
- 15-20 min at summit (if visiting)
- 30-40 min on 1st floor (most spacious, most to see)
- Add 30-45 min if having a snack or meal
- Plan additional 30-45 min for Trocadero photo stop (can be before or after the tower visit)

## 8. Practical Tips Summary for the Travel Guide

1. **Book elevator tickets online 60 days in advance** - this is essential to avoid 1-2 hour ground queues
2. **Go early morning (9:30 AM) or evening** for fewer crowds
3. **Dress warmly** - even in April, the upper levels are windy and cold (bring a windbreaker)
4. **Combine with Trocadero** - walk across to Trocadero before or after for the best photo of the tower
5. **Allow 2.5-3 hours** for the complete experience
6. **Nearest Metro:** Bir-Hakeim (Line 6) or Trocadero (Lines 6, 9)
7. **Nearest RER:** Champ de Mars-Tour Eiffel (RER C)
8. **No large luggage** allowed; no left luggage facility
9. **The tower sparkles** every hour on the hour after dark for 5 minutes - worth seeing from outside
10. **The 1st floor glass floor** is a highlight - don't skip it even if you visit the summit
