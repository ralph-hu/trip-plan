## 1. Colosseum (Colosseo / 斗兽场)

### Visiting Route & Levels

**Standard ticket** grants access to:
- **Ground floor (1st level)**: Entry via the Sperone Valadier Entrance (main entrance, near the Arch of Constantine). This is where you enter and begin your visit. Exhibits and information points are located here.
- **Second floor (2nd level)**: Walk up to the spectator level, where ancient Rome's elite would have sat. You can walk around the full amphitheater perimeter, looking down over the arena. Small exhibits and information panels are displayed here. This offers the classic panoramic view down into the arena and the exposed Hypogeum tunnels below.

**Full Experience ticket (EUR 24)** adds access to:
- **Arena floor**: Walk through the Gate of Death (Porta Libitinaria), where fallen gladiators were carried out. From the arena floor, you look directly down into the Hypogeum and get a ground-level perspective of the towering walls.
- **Underground / Hypogeum (地下通道)**: The labyrinth of tunnels beneath the arena floor. This is where gladiators prepared and wild animals were caged. You can see trap doors and lift mechanisms that raised animals into the arena. Fully opened to visitors since 2021.

**Standard visiting path**: Enter at ground level --> explore ground floor exhibits --> ascend to 2nd floor --> walk the perimeter viewing the arena from above --> descend and exit. With Full Experience: add arena floor and underground before or after the upper levels.

### Key Viewpoints & Photo Spots Inside

1. **2nd floor perimeter (north side)**: The classic "looking down into the arena" shot with the Hypogeum visible. Best overall interior photo.
2. **Arena floor** (Full Experience only): Standing where gladiators fought, looking up at the towering tiers -- dramatic perspective photos.
3. **Gate of Death**: Walk through the actual gate; atmospheric photo opportunity.
4. **View toward Arch of Constantine**: From the 2nd floor, you can frame the Arch of Constantine and Palatine Hill through the Colosseum arches.

### Exterior Photo Spots
- **North side**: The intact, most impressive facade -- 57 meters tall, travertine marble. Best for the classic full-building photo.
- **With Arch of Constantine**: Stand between the Colosseum and the Arch for photos that capture both monuments.

### Combined Ticket: Colosseum + Roman Forum + Palatine Hill

All standard Colosseum tickets include access to Roman Forum and Palatine Hill. Tickets go on sale 30 days before visit date and can sell out weeks ahead.

**Recommended time split**:
- Colosseum: 1-1.5 hours (standard ticket) / 2 hours (Full Experience with arena + underground)
- Roman Forum: 1.5 hours
- Palatine Hill: 45 min-1 hour
- **Total combined: 3.5-4.5 hours**

### Roman Forum (古罗马广场) - Key Ruins & Walking Path

**Suggested walking route** (from Colosseum entrance side heading west):

1. **Arch of Titus (提图斯凯旋门)**: First major monument as you enter from the Colosseum side. Built AD 81 to commemorate the conquest of Jerusalem. Reliefs inside the arch show the spoils being carried in triumph.

2. **Temple of Venus and Roma**: Near the Colosseum end, one of the largest temples of ancient Rome.

3. **Basilica of Maxentius (马克森提乌斯大殿)**: Enormous ruined hall -- three massive barrel-vaulted bays remain. One of the largest buildings in the Forum.

4. **Temple of Antoninus and Faustina**: Well-preserved due to conversion into a church. The original columns still stand.

5. **Temple of the Vestal Virgins (维斯塔贞女庙)**: The round temple and the House of the Vestals -- an atmospheric courtyard with statues.

6. **Via Sacra (Sacred Way / 神圣大道)**: The main ancient road running through the Forum. Walk along it.

7. **Temple of Saturn (萨图恩神庙)**: Eight remaining columns at the western end. One of the oldest and most iconic ruins. Also served as the Roman treasury.

8. **Arch of Septimius Severus (塞维鲁凯旋门)**: Triple arch at the western end, well-preserved with detailed battle relief panels.

9. **Rostra (演讲台)**: The platform from which Roman orators addressed the public.

**Duration**: Plan at least 90 minutes. The ground is uneven (cobblestones), and there is very little shade on sunny days. Bring water and wear comfortable shoes.

**Elderly visitor note**: The Forum paths are uneven ancient cobblestones. Take it slowly. There are no elevators. Palatine Hill involves climbing a moderate hillside path.

### Palatine Hill (帕拉蒂尼山)

From the Forum, take the path leading uphill. This was where Rome's emperors built their palaces.
- **Top viewpoint**: Offers the best panoramic view of the entire Forum, with the Colosseum framed in the background. This is the money shot.
- Green, peaceful gardens contrast with the Forum ruins below.
- Legend says this is where Romulus and Remus founded Rome.
- **Elderly note**: Moderate uphill walk required. Worth it for the view if mobility allows; skip if stairs are difficult.

## 2. Vatican Museums + Sistine Chapel + St. Peter's Basilica (梵蒂冈)

### Vatican Museums (梵蒂冈博物馆)

**Hours**: Monday-Saturday, 09:00-18:00 (last admission 16:00). Closed Sundays (except last Sunday of month, when free but extremely crowded).

**Getting there**: Metro Line A to "Ottaviano" station, then 10-minute walk along Via Ottaviano/Via Germanico to Viale Vaticano. The museum entrance is on Viale Vaticano (NOT the same as St. Peter's Square entrance).

**Total museum length**: Over 7 km / 14 km of galleries if you see everything.

### Recommended Route (2-3 hour highlights)

The museums follow a largely one-way system. Here is the priority route:

1. **Pinecone Courtyard (Cortile della Pigna / 松果庭院)** -- Starting point after entry
   - "Sphere Within a Sphere" bronze sculpture by Arnaldo Pomodoro
   - Good place to orient yourself; outdoor benches for rest

2. **Octagon Courtyard / Pio-Clementino Museum**
   - **Laocoon Group (拉奥孔)**: Greek sculpture from ~40 BCE showing a priest and his sons being attacked by serpents. Stunning facial expressions.
   - **Belvedere Torso (观景台躯干)**: Fragment of a Greek sculpture that Michelangelo studied obsessively; influenced his Sistine Chapel figures.
   - **Apollo Belvedere**: Iconic classical statue.

3. **Gallery of the Candelabra** --> **Gallery of Tapestries** --> **Gallery of Maps (地图廊)**
   - **Gallery of Maps**: 40 topographical maps of Italian regions painted in 1583. Remarkably accurate. Maps are oriented from Rome's perspective, so some regions appear "upside down." One of the most visually striking rooms.

4. **Raphael Rooms (拉斐尔画室)** -- 4 rooms total
   - **Room of the Segnatura**: Contains "The School of Athens" (雅典学院), Raphael's masterpiece. Central figures are Plato and Aristotle. Raphael snuck in Michelangelo as a cameo (the figure in pale lilac, sitting pensively). Completed 1511.
   - Also contains frescoes on theology, law, and poetry.

5. **Borgia Apartments**: Six ornately decorated rooms with frescoes by Pinturicchio (late 15th century).

6. **Sistine Chapel (西斯廷教堂)** -- the culmination of the museum route

### Sistine Chapel (西斯廷教堂)

**Location**: At the end of the Vatican Museums route. You must walk through the museums to reach it.

**What to look for on the ceiling**:
- Michelangelo painted the ceiling between 1508-1512
- **Nine central panels** depict scenes from Genesis, running from the altar to the entrance:
  - **The Creation of Adam (创造亚当)**: The most famous panel -- God reaching out to touch Adam's finger. Located in the center of the ceiling. Look for the gap between their fingers.
  - **The Creation of Eve**
  - **The Fall and Expulsion from Eden**
  - **The Separation of Light from Darkness**
  - **The Creation of the Sun and Moon**
  - **The Deluge (Noah's Flood)**
- Surrounding the central panels: **Prophets and Sibyls** seated on thrones
- **Ignudi**: 20 athletic nude male figures framing the central panels

**The Last Judgment (最后的审判)** -- altar wall:
- Painted by Michelangelo 1536-1541, over 20 years after the ceiling
- Massive fresco covering the entire altar wall
- Christ in the center as judge, surrounded by saints
- The damned are dragged down to Hell on the right side
- Michelangelo painted his own face on the flayed skin held by St. Bartholomew (a self-portrait expressing his suffering)
- Originally all figures were nude; draperies were added later by order of Pope Paul IV

**Rules**:
- **No photography** inside the Sistine Chapel (strictly enforced)
- **No talking** (guards shush visitors -- "Silenzio!")
- Dress code: shoulders and knees must be covered

### Shortcut from Sistine Chapel to St. Peter's Basilica

**Yes, a side door exists** on the right side of the Sistine Chapel that leads directly into St. Peter's Basilica. However:
- This door is officially reserved for **guided tour groups** -- guides with licensed Vatican tour credentials can take their groups through this door.
- Individual visitors using standard tickets are directed to exit back through the museum galleries and walk around to St. Peter's Square (approximately 15-minute walk outside).
- **Recommendation**: Book a guided tour that includes both Vatican Museums and St. Peter's Basilica -- this typically grants access through the side door, saving significant time and the 15-minute outdoor walk.

### St. Peter's Basilica (圣彼得大教堂)

**Entry**: FREE. No ticket required for the basilica itself. However, expect security lines of 20-40 minutes (longer in peak season).

**Hours**: Generally 07:00-18:30 (hours may vary seasonally).

**What to see inside**:

1. **La Pieta (圣殇) by Michelangelo** -- First chapel on the right as you enter
   - Completed in 1499 when Michelangelo was just 24
   - Now behind bulletproof glass (attacked with a hammer in 1972, later restored)
   - Note Mary's face -- art historians suggest it may be modeled on Michelangelo's mother, who died when he was young
   - The only work Michelangelo ever signed (on the sash across Mary's chest)

2. **Baldacchino di San Pietro (圣彼得华盖)** by Bernini
   - Enormous bronze canopy, 20 meters (66 feet) tall, over the high altar
   - Marks the location directly above St. Peter's tomb
   - Completed in 1634; bronze was taken from the Pantheon's porch

3. **St. Peter's Tomb**: Visible through a glass screen beneath the altar. The Papal Grottoes below contain tombs of many popes.

4. **Ancient bronze statue of St. Peter**: Right foot visibly worn down from centuries of pilgrims touching/kissing it.

5. **Tomb of Pope Alexander VII** by Bernini: Features a gilded bronze skeleton holding an hourglass, draped in carved jasper fabric.

### Dome Climb (登顶穹顶)

- **Elevator option**: Takes you to the roof/terrace level (saves 231 steps). From there, you must still climb 320 steps through an increasingly narrow and curving staircase to reach the very top.
- **Total steps without elevator**: 551 steps
- **Total steps with elevator**: 320 steps (still significant)
- **Elderly suitability**: NOT recommended for elderly visitors with mobility issues. The upper staircase is very narrow, the walls curve inward following the dome's shape, and it can be claustrophobic. There is no way to turn back easily once you start.
- **View from top**: Panoramic 360-degree view of Rome and St. Peter's Square. Spectacular, but the physical demands are significant.
- **Alternative**: The view from the roof terrace (elevator level) is already quite good and avoids the difficult final climb.

### St. Peter's Square (圣彼得广场)

- Designed by Bernini, completed 1667
- Elliptical shape, 240 meters wide
- 284 columns in 4 rows forming the iconic colonnade
- **Best viewpoints**:
  1. **From the dome** (if you climb): Looking straight down onto the square and Via della Conciliazione
  2. **Center of the square**: Look for the round stone markers on the ground -- standing on these specific spots, the 4 rows of columns appear to align perfectly into a single row (Bernini's optical illusion)
  3. **From Via della Conciliazione**: The classic straight-on approach view with the basilica dome centered above the square

### Suggested Duration -- Full Vatican Visit

- Vatican Museums + Sistine Chapel (guided tour): 2-2.5 hours
- Break/rest in Pinecone Courtyard: 15-30 minutes
- St. Peter's Basilica (interior only, no dome): 45 min-1 hour
- St. Peter's Square: 15-20 minutes
- **Total: 4-5 hours (full day recommended)**

## 3. Spanish Steps & Trevi Fountain (西班牙台阶 & 许愿泉)

### Spanish Steps (Scalinata di Trinita dei Monti / 西班牙台阶)

**Key facts**:
- 135 steps connecting Piazza di Spagna (bottom) to the Trinita dei Monti church (top)
- Built 1723-1725; one of Rome's most iconic landmarks
- Free to visit; open 24 hours
- **Note**: Sitting on the steps is PROHIBITED (since 2019). Fines can be issued. You can walk up and down but cannot sit.
- At the bottom: the Fontana della Barcaccia (Fountain of the Old Boat) by Bernini's father, Pietro Bernini

**Best time to visit**:
- **Early morning (07:00-08:00)**: Fewest crowds. The steps face roughly west, so morning light illuminates the church at the top nicely.
- **Late evening (after 21:00)**: Beautiful when lit up at night, and crowds thin considerably.
- **Avoid**: 10:00-17:00 is peak tourist congestion.
- **Spring (April)**: The steps are decorated with azalea flowers, making them especially photogenic -- perfect timing for your trip.

**Photo tips**:
1. **From the bottom of the steps** looking up: Frame the Trinita dei Monti church centered above the steps with the obelisk.
2. **From the top of the steps** looking down: Panoramic view over Piazza di Spagna and the Roman rooftops stretching toward St. Peter's dome in the distance.
3. **From the side** (Via dei Condotti): Classic angled view showing the sweep of the staircase.
- **Elderly note**: 135 steps is a significant climb. The top can be reached alternatively by walking around via Via Gregoriana or Via Sistina. Consider taking the Metro (Line A, "Spagna" station exits right at the bottom).

### Trevi Fountain (Fontana di Trevi / 许愿泉)

**Key facts**:
- Largest Baroque fountain in Rome: 26 meters high, 20 meters wide
- Completed 1762 by architect Nicola Salvi (and later Giuseppe Pannini)
- The terminal point of the ancient Acqua Vergine aqueduct (dating to 19 BCE)
- Free to visit; open 24 hours

**Best time to visit**:
- **Late evening / night (after 22:00-23:00)**: The fountain is beautifully illuminated with warm yellow lighting. Crowds are much thinner. Multiple sources recommend this as the absolute best time.
- **Very early morning (before 08:00)**: If you prefer daylight, arrive before 8 AM.
- **Avoid**: 09:00-22:00 is almost always extremely crowded. The Trevi Fountain area is chaotic during these hours.

**Coin-throwing tradition**:
- Use your **right hand**, throw the coin **over your left shoulder** with your back to the fountain
- 1 coin = you will return to Rome
- 2 coins = you will fall in love
- 3 coins = you will marry
- About EUR 1 million in coins is collected annually; donated to Caritas charity

**What to notice in the sculptures**:
- **Central figure**: This is the Titan **Oceanus** (often mistakenly called Neptune), riding a shell chariot
- **Two sea horses (hippocampi)**: One calm, one wild -- representing the changing moods of the sea
- **Two tritons** guiding the horses
- **Flanking niches**: Statues of Abundance (basket of fruit) and Salubrity (laurel wreath)
- **Four goddesses on top**: Representing the earth's bounty from water (fruit, crops, harvest, flowers)
- **Two relief panels**: One shows the virgin showing Romans where to find pure water; the other shows Marcus Agrippa ordering aqueduct construction

**Photo tips**:
1. **Elevated view from United Colors of Benetton store**: The store facing the fountain has a 2nd floor with windows overlooking the fountain -- an uncrowded vantage point to see the whole composition.
2. **From the left side** (as you face the fountain): Less crowded than dead-center; captures the fountain at a flattering angle.
3. **Night photos**: The warm lighting creates dramatic contrast. Use a phone's night mode for best results.
- **Elderly note**: The square around the fountain is small and very crowded. Watch for pickpockets. The fountain is at street level (no stairs required). Walking from Spanish Steps to Trevi Fountain takes about 10 minutes.

## Summary: Recommended Daily Schedule for Rome (Elderly-Friendly)

### Day 1: Colosseum Area (half day)
- 08:30 Enter Colosseum (book Full Experience ticket in advance)
- 08:30-10:30 Colosseum (2 hours with arena + underground)
- 10:30-12:00 Roman Forum (1.5 hours, walk slowly on uneven ground)
- 12:00-12:45 Palatine Hill (if mobility permits; skip if tired)
- Afternoon: Rest, or visit Spanish Steps + Trevi Fountain

### Day 2: Vatican (full day)
- 08:00 Arrive at Vatican Museums entrance (Viale Vaticano)
- 09:00-11:30 Vatican Museums guided tour --> Sistine Chapel
- 11:30-12:00 Rest in Pinecone Courtyard
- 12:00-13:00 Walk to St. Peter's Basilica (use guide shortcut if available, otherwise 15-min walk outside)
- 13:00-14:00 St. Peter's Basilica interior
- 14:00-14:20 St. Peter's Square
- Skip dome climb (not suitable for elderly)

### Evening: Spanish Steps + Trevi Fountain
- Best visited after 21:00 for fewer crowds and beautiful lighting
- Walk from Spanish Steps to Trevi Fountain: ~10 minutes
- Total time needed: 45 min-1 hour for both
