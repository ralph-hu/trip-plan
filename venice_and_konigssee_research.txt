FAMILY TRIP RESEARCH: VENICE & KONIGSSEE
=========================================
(3 people, including elderly parents)
Note: Based on knowledge through May 2025. Verify prices/schedules on official sites before travel.

================================================================================
1. VENICE (2 DAYS)
================================================================================

--- DAY 1: ARRIVAL EVENING ---

What to do:
- Leisurely walk from your hotel toward San Marco area; Venice is magical at dusk
  with fewer crowds
- Get a gelato and stroll along the waterfront (Riva degli Schiavoni)
- Light dinner at a bacaro (Venetian tapas bar) near Rialto -- casual, quick, authentic

Best area for gondola ride at sunset:
- BEST: Start near the Rialto Bridge area or from the San Marco basin
  - The route along the Grand Canal near Rialto gives you views of the palazzi
    glowing in sunset light
  - Alternatively, starting from near San Marco and going through smaller canals
    toward the Grand Canal offers a mix of intimate canals + open water sunset views
- The Bacino di San Marco (St. Mark's Basin) offers the widest open-water sunset
  panorama -- San Giorgio Maggiore island silhouetted at sunset is iconic
- TIP: Official gondola rate is ~80 EUR for 30 min (daytime), ~100 EUR after 7 PM.
  Price is per gondola (up to 6 people), NOT per person. Negotiate before boarding.
  There is no need to pay more unless you want singing/musicians.
- For elderly parents: boarding a gondola can be slightly tricky (stepping down into
  the boat). The gondolier will assist. Choose a well-maintained station.

--- DAY 2: FULL DAY ---

Recommended itinerary & time for each attraction:

  08:30 - St. Mark's Square (Piazza San Marco) -- 45 min to 1 hr
    - Walk the square, admire the Basilica facade, the Campanile, the Clock Tower
    - St. Mark's Basilica: free entry to the main nave (15-20 min inside)
      Skip-the-line tickets available online (~3 EUR reservation fee)
    - Campanile (bell tower): ~10 EUR, great views but involves elevator. 15-20 min.
    - Best to arrive early (before 9:30) to beat crowds

  09:30 - Doge's Palace (Palazzo Ducale) -- 1.5 to 2 hrs
    - Allow time for the grand staircase, council chambers, Bridge of Sighs
    - Pace slowly for elderly parents; there are many stairs inside
    - See ticket info below

  11:30 - Walk to Rialto Bridge -- 15 min walk, 30 min at the bridge
    - Cross the bridge, browse the market stalls
    - Rialto fish/produce market (mornings only, closed Sundays)

  12:00 - Lunch near Rialto (see restaurant suggestions below)

  14:00 - Boat to Burano -- approx. 40-45 min by vaporetto
    - Take Vaporetto Line 12 from Fondamente Nove (not from Rialto directly)
    - Walk from Rialto to Fondamente Nove: ~15 min, or take vaporetto line 5.2
    - Burano: 1.5 to 2 hrs is plenty
      - Colorful houses for photos, lace shops, a gelato
      - Elderly-friendly: flat terrain, small island, benches available
    - Return by ~17:00-17:30

  17:30 - Return to Venice, rest or final evening stroll

  TOTAL Vaporetto tip: Buy a 24-hr or 48-hr vaporetto pass (~21 EUR / ~29 EUR).
  Single rides are 9.50 EUR each -- pass pays for itself in 3 rides.

--- DOGE'S PALACE TICKETS ---

  YES, you need tickets. Pre-booking strongly recommended (especially spring/summer).

  Standard ticket (Musei di Piazza San Marco):
  - Adult: ~30 EUR (as of 2024/2025 -- verify on palazzoducale.visitmuve.it)
  - Reduced (students, seniors 65+): ~22 EUR -- CHECK if your parents qualify
  - This combined ticket includes: Doge's Palace + Museo Correr +
    Museo Archeologico + Biblioteca Marciana
  - Children under 5: free

  Book at: https://palazzoducale.visitmuve.it/en/plan-your-visit/tickets/

  "Secret Itineraries" guided tour (~28 EUR) visits hidden rooms, torture chambers,
  Casanova's cell -- fascinating but involves narrow passages and stairs, may not
  suit elderly visitors.

--- RECOMMENDED SEAFOOD RESTAURANTS (mid-range, local favorites) ---

  1. Trattoria alla Madonna (near Rialto)
     - Classic Venetian seafood, family-run since 1954
     - Mid-range (~25-40 EUR/person)
     - No reservations -- arrive early (12:00 for lunch, 19:00 for dinner)

  2. Osteria alle Testiere (near Campo Santa Maria Formosa)
     - Small (10 tables), reservation essential
     - Excellent fresh fish, daily-changing menu
     - ~40-50 EUR/person
     - One of the best seafood spots in Venice

  3. Trattoria da Romano (on Burano island)
     - If you visit Burano, eat here -- famous for risotto di go (goby fish risotto)
     - Mid-range, ~30-40 EUR/person
     - Has been serving since 1920s

  4. Al Covo (Castello district)
     - Husband-wife run, locally sourced seafood
     - ~35-50 EUR/person

  5. Anice Stellato (Cannaregio, away from tourist center)
     - Locals' favorite, excellent value
     - ~25-35 EUR/person

  AVOID: Restaurants with "tourist menu" signs, those right on St. Mark's Square
  (overpriced), and places with photo menus outside.

--- ELDERLY-SPECIFIC TIPS FOR VENICE ---
  - Venice has MANY bridges with steps and no ramps -- plan routes carefully
  - Vaporetto (water bus) is the easiest transport; sitting is available
  - Water taxis are expensive (~70-100 EUR) but door-to-door, good for tired parents
  - Stay near San Marco or Rialto to minimize walking
  - Bring comfortable shoes; cobblestones are uneven


================================================================================
2. KONIGSSEE (KING'S LAKE), GERMANY
================================================================================

--- TOTAL VISIT DURATION ---

  Plan 4 to 6 hours total:
  - Parking + walk to boat dock: 15 min
  - Boat to St. Bartholomä: ~35 min one way
  - Time at St. Bartholomä: 30-60 min (church, lakeshore walk)
  - Boat St. Bartholomä to Salet (for Obersee): ~20 min
  - Walk from Salet to Obersee lake: 15-20 min (flat, easy gravel path)
  - Time at Obersee: 30-60 min
  - Return boat from Salet to Schönau: ~55 min
  - Total: roughly 4-6 hours depending on pace

  For elderly parents: Consider going only to St. Bartholomä (round trip ~2.5 hrs
  total). The Obersee walk is flat but adds significant time. The Salet-Obersee
  path is gravel and manageable for moderately mobile elderly.

--- KEY HIGHLIGHTS ---

  St. Bartholomä:
  - Iconic onion-domed chapel on the lakeshore (photo-perfect)
  - Small restaurant serving fresh smoked trout from the lake -- highly recommended
  - Easy, flat area around the chapel
  - Free to enter the chapel

  Obersee:
  - Smaller, crystal-clear alpine lake behind Königssee
  - 15-20 min flat walk from Salet boat stop
  - Stunning scenery: Röthbachfall waterfall visible in the distance (Germany's
    highest waterfall at 470m)
  - The walk is on a wide gravel path, stroller/wheelchair partially accessible

  The boat ride itself:
  - Electric boats (silent, eco-friendly) -- gliding through fjord-like scenery
  - Halfway point: the boatman plays a trumpet/flugelhorn to demonstrate the echo
    off the cliff walls -- a memorable moment
  - Crystal-clear water (drinking quality)

--- PARKING AND LOGISTICS (DRIVING) ---

  Main parking: Königssee parking lots in Schönau am Königssee
  - Large parking area ~5 min walk from the boat dock
  - Cost: ~5-7 EUR/day (varies by lot; P1 is closest)
  - Arrives early in peak season (by 9:00-9:30 AM)
  - In early April: parking should be easy, off-peak

  Address for GPS: Seestraße, 83471 Schönau am Königssee

  From the parking lot, walk through a pleasant pedestrian street with shops and
  cafes to reach the boat dock (~5-10 min walk, flat).

--- IS THE BOAT RUNNING IN EARLY APRIL? ---

  CRITICAL CHECK: The boat schedule is seasonal.

  - Boats to St. Bartholomä: Run YEAR-ROUND (weather permitting)
  - Boats to Salet/Obersee: Typically START mid/late April to mid-October

  In early April, you can almost certainly reach St. Bartholomä, but the Salet
  stop (for Obersee) is very likely NOT yet operational.

  >>> VERIFY the exact 2026 schedule at:
      https://www.bayerische-seenschifffahrt.de/en/koenigssee/
      or call: +49 8652 96360

  Winter/early spring schedule: fewer departures, typically every 1-1.5 hours
  (vs. every 30 min in summer). First boat around 9:45, last return around 16:30.

  Weather note: Early April can still be cold (2-10C), occasional snow possible.
  Dress in layers.

--- RAMSAU BEI BERCHTESGADEN -- WORTH A STOP? ---

  YES, absolutely worth a 30-60 minute stop. It is on the way to/from Königssee.

  Highlights:
  - Parish Church of St. Sebastian: the most photographed church in Germany,
    with the Reiteralpe mountains behind it and the Ramsauer Ache stream in front
  - The "Malerwinkel" (Painter's Corner) viewpoint of the church is a 2-min walk
  - Zauberwald (Magic Forest): short, flat walking path through mossy forest --
    beautiful but may be partially muddy in early April
  - Hintersee lake: small alpine lake ~5 min drive beyond Ramsau, very pretty,
    with a flat lakeside walk (15-20 min loop)

  For elderly parents: The church viewpoint is right off the road (almost no
  walking). Hintersee has a paved/flat path around parts of the lake. Very
  manageable.

  Suggested timing: Stop in Ramsau BEFORE Königssee (it's on the way if coming
  from Salzburg/Munich direction). Spend 30-45 min for photos at the church +
  quick Hintersee visit.

================================================================================

  08:30  Depart accommodation
  09:00  Ramsau - St. Sebastian church photo stop + Hintersee (45 min)
  10:00  Drive to Königssee parking (10 min)

  10:45  Boat to St. Bartholomä
  11:20  Arrive St. Bartholomä -- visit chapel, eat smoked trout
  12:30  Return boat to Schönau
  13:05  Arrive back, browse shops
