# Drone Regulations Guide for Chinese Tourist (DJI Consumer Drone)
# 无人机携带与飞行法规指南 — 法德瑞意四国

**Applicable dates:** 2026.3.31 - 4.13
**Drone type assumed:** DJI consumer drone (e.g., DJI Mini 4 Pro <249g, or DJI Air series ~595-720g)
**Operator nationality:** Chinese citizen (non-EU)

> **IMPORTANT NOTE ON DRONE WEIGHT:** If you bring a sub-249g drone (DJI Mini series), some registration and insurance requirements are lighter. If your drone is 250g+, nearly all requirements apply in full. The DJI Mini 4 Pro (249g) sits right on the boundary — some countries treat it as 250g class. Check your specific model's weight carefully.

---

## Overall EU Framework (Applies to France, Germany, Italy)

The EU uses a harmonized drone regulation system under EASA (European Union Aviation Safety Agency):

- **Open Category** is what recreational/tourist flights fall under
  - **A1 subcategory:** Fly over people (drones <250g only)
  - **A2 subcategory:** Fly close to people (requires additional certificate)
  - **A3 subcategory:** Fly far from people (drones <25kg, must stay 150m from residential/commercial/industrial areas)
- **Maximum altitude:** 120 meters (400 feet) above ground level
- **Visual Line of Sight (VLOS)** required at all times
- **Non-EU tourists** must register as a UAS operator in whichever EU country they first fly in
- **Each country adds its own national rules** on top of the EU framework
- **Switzerland** is NOT in the EU but follows EASA rules through bilateral agreements

## 1. FRANCE

### Can tourists fly drones?
Yes, legally permitted — but with very heavy restrictions that make tourist flying in Paris completely impossible.

### Registration Requirements
- **Register on AlphaTango** (France's official portal): Free, ~15 minutes online
  - URL: https://alphatango.aviation-civile.gouv.fr/
  - You receive an operator ID in format "FRA" + 13 characters
  - This ID must be physically displayed on the drone
  - Registration valid for 5 years
- **A1/A3 online training** required if drone is 250g+
- Chinese citizens can register — same process as all non-EU operators
- Your Chinese drone license/registration is NOT recognized

### Insurance
- **Mandatory** — third-party liability insurance required regardless of drone size
- Must be valid in France; arrange before traveling

### France-Specific Rule: 800g Beacon
- Drones over 800g require an electronic WiFi beacon (balise electronique) — a France-only rule
- DJI Mini series and Air series are under 800g, so this does NOT apply to most consumer drones

### No-Fly Zones Relevant to Your Itinerary

| Location | Status | Details |
|---|---|---|
| **All of central Paris** | **COMPLETELY BANNED** | LF-P 23 is a permanent prohibited zone from ground to 6,500 ft covering ALL arrondissements |
| **Eiffel Tower** | BANNED | Within Paris no-fly zone |
| **Louvre** | BANNED | Within Paris no-fly zone |
| **Notre-Dame** | BANNED | Within Paris no-fly zone; tourists have been arrested here |
| **Montmartre** | BANNED | Within Paris no-fly zone |
| **Arc de Triomphe** | BANNED | Within Paris no-fly zone |
| **Versailles** | BANNED | Versailles has its own restrictions; also near Paris zone |
| **All 11 national parks** | BANNED | Recreational drones prohibited |

### Night Flying
**Completely banned** for recreational pilots — no exceptions, even with lights.

### Penalties
- Flying in prohibited zones: **up to 1 year imprisonment + EUR 75,000 fine**
- Privacy violations (filming private premises): **1 year prison + EUR 45,000 fine**
- These are CRIMINAL penalties, not just administrative fines

### Where You COULD Realistically Fly
- Rural countryside areas away from airports, cities, and populated zones
- You would need to check the **Geoportail map** before every flight
- Realistically for your itinerary: **there is NO practical place to fly in France** since your entire stay is in Paris and Versailles

### Apps & Websites
- **Geoportail map:** https://www.geoportail.gouv.fr/donnees/restrictions-uas-categorie-ouverte-et-aeromodelisme (official, authoritative)
- **AlphaTango registration:** https://alphatango.aviation-civile.gouv.fr/

### Practical Assessment: NOT RECOMMENDED for your France itinerary
Your entire France segment is Paris + Versailles = 100% no-fly zone. Do not bring the drone out in France.

## 2. GERMANY

### Can tourists fly drones?
Yes, with registration and insurance. More feasible than France in some areas, but key itinerary spots are restricted.

### Registration Requirements
- **Register on DIPUL portal:** EUR 20 fee
  - URL: https://www.dipul.de/homepage/en/
  - Operated by LBA (German Federal Aviation Office)
- **A1/A3 certificate exam** required online
- **Any drone with a camera** requires registration, regardless of weight — even a sub-250g DJI Mini with a camera
- Chinese citizens can register as non-EU operators

### Insurance
- **Mandatory** — no exceptions
- Minimum coverage: 750,000 SDR (~EUR 940,000)
- Budget EUR 50-100 for short-term EU drone liability policy; arrange before travel

### No-Fly Zones Relevant to Your Itinerary

| Location | Status | Details |
|---|---|---|
| **Cologne Cathedral** | RESTRICTED | In urban city center; flying over gatherings of people prohibited; cathedral likely under cultural heritage protection |
| **Munich city center** | RESTRICTED | Urban area restrictions; cannot fly over residential properties with camera without consent |
| **Neuschwanstein Castle** | LIKELY RESTRICTED | Royal castle, cultural heritage site; likely has local flight restrictions. Check DIPUL map |
| **Berchtesgaden National Park** | **COMPLETELY BANNED** | All 16 German national parks prohibit recreational drone flights (since 2017) |
| **Konigssee** | **BANNED** | Within Berchtesgaden National Park boundaries |
| **All Natura 2000 areas** | BANNED | Protected nature areas throughout Germany |

### Night Flying
Permitted with a green flashing anti-collision light (more lenient than France).

### Penalties
- Administrative fines: EUR 500 to EUR 50,000
- Endangering air traffic: up to 10 years imprisonment

### Where You COULD Realistically Fly
- Rural areas outside cities, national parks, and nature reserves
- Must stay below 120m altitude, maintain VLOS
- Open fields, farmland between cities — if not in a restricted zone
- **Possible:** Open countryside while driving between Munich and Neuschwanstein (pull over in a rural area, check the app, fly briefly)

### Apps & Websites
- **DIPUL registration portal:** https://www.dipul.de/homepage/en/
- **DFS DrohnenApp** (mobile app): Real-time restriction zone checking — download before travel
- **DFS website:** https://www.dfs.de/homepage/en/drone-flight/

### Practical Assessment: MARGINALLY POSSIBLE
Most of your Germany itinerary is urban (Cologne, Munich) or national park (Berchtesgaden). Your only realistic window is rural countryside during the driving segments. The registration cost (EUR 20) and insurance burden make this a lot of hassle for limited opportunity.

## 3. SWITZERLAND

### Can tourists fly drones?
Yes, with registration. Switzerland follows EASA rules but has its own registration system. The Alps offer stunning drone footage potential, BUT popular tourist areas are heavily restricted.

### Registration Requirements
- **Register on FOCA dLIS platform:** Free
  - Requires a Swiss eIAM, AGOV, or CH-Login account
  - Must pass A1/A3 online exam (~4 hours)
- Chinese citizens can register as non-EU operators

### Insurance
- **Mandatory for drones 250g+ or with a camera**
- Minimum coverage: CHF 1,000,000 per incident
- **Critical issue:** Swiss insurers typically do NOT cover foreign tourists — you must arrange coverage in your home country before traveling

### No-Fly Zones Relevant to Your Itinerary

| Location | Status | Details |
|---|---|---|
| **Grindelwald** | HEAVILY RESTRICTED | Helicopter routes (Air-Glaciers, Rega rescue) create restriction zones; local village may have bans |
| **Jungfraujoch** | EFFECTIVELY BANNED | High-altitude area with helicopter traffic, military training zones, and extreme conditions; impractical and likely restricted |
| **Lauterbrunnen** | RESTRICTED | Popular tourist valley with helicopter traffic |
| **Interlaken area** | RESTRICTED | Military training areas create significant restricted airspace in Bernese Oberland |
| **Swiss National Park** (Engadin) | BANNED | Complete drone ban |
| **Major city airports** | 5km no-fly zones around Zurich, Geneva, Basel |

### Privacy Laws
Switzerland uses its own privacy law (DSG/FADP, not GDPR). Article 13 FADP specifically addresses aerial photography — consent required to record identifiable persons.

### Penalties
- Standard violations: CHF 150 - 20,000
- Aggravated cases: up to CHF 40,000

### Where You COULD Realistically Fly
- Rural alpine valleys outside national parks, nature reserves, and helicopter zones
- Eastern Switzerland and Jura mountains tend to have fewer restrictions
- **For your itinerary (Grindelwald/Jungfraujoch):** Very limited. The Bernese Oberland has extensive helicopter routes and military zones. You would need to find a spot away from villages, helicopter paths, and protected areas — difficult in practice.

### Apps & Websites
- **FOCA dLIS registration:** Check FOCA's official website
- **FOCA drone rules page:** https://www.bazl.admin.ch/bazl/en/home/good-to-know/drones-and-aircraft-models.html
- **Check local canton websites** for area-specific rules (restrictions are often NOT visible on national airspace maps)

### Practical Assessment: DIFFICULT BUT POSSIBLE IN SOME SPOTS
Grindelwald and Jungfraujoch are your target areas, and both are heavily restricted. If you can find an open alpine meadow away from villages and helicopter routes, brief flights might be possible. But the registration process, insurance requirement, and area restrictions make this challenging.

## 4. ITALY

### Can tourists fly drones?
Yes, but with the most expensive registration of all four countries, and heavy restrictions around major cities.

### Registration Requirements
- **Register on D-Flight platform:** EUR 90 one-time fee + EUR 6/month subscription
  - Operated by ENAV (Italian air navigation provider)
- Must pass A1/A3 online exam
- Chinese citizens can register as non-EU operators

### Insurance
- **Mandatory** — even for sub-250g drones (stricter than most EU countries)
- Minimum coverage: ~EUR 920,000 per incident
- Must be valid in Italy

### No-Fly Zones Relevant to Your Itinerary

| Location | Status | Details |
|---|---|---|
| **Venice — entire city** | **COMPLETELY BANNED** | Canals, St. Mark's Square, Rialto Bridge: absolute prohibition. Marco Polo Airport restricted zone covers much of the lagoon |
| **Rome historic center** | **COMPLETELY BANNED** | Permanent no-fly zone LI P244 on D-Flight |
| **Colosseum** | BANNED | Within Rome's historic center no-fly zone |
| **Vatican** | BANNED | Vatican airspace is restricted (sovereign state) |
| **Roman Forum** | BANNED | Within Rome's historic center no-fly zone |
| **Trevi Fountain** | BANNED | Within Rome's historic center no-fly zone |
| **Spanish Steps** | BANNED | Within Rome's historic center no-fly zone |
| **All 25 national parks** | BANNED | Law 394/1991 prohibits drones in all national parks, regional parks, nature reserves, and Natura 2000 sites |
| **59 UNESCO heritage sites** | RESTRICTED | Most require special permits from cultural authorities |

### Penalties
- Flying in prohibited zones: **up to 2 years imprisonment + fines up to EUR 64,000**
- Criminal offenses under Italy's navigation code

### Where You COULD Realistically Fly
- Rural Tuscany, coastal areas away from airports
- Dolomites (outside protected zones)
- Rural areas between Venice and Rome — but ALWAYS check D-Flight first
- **For your itinerary:** Venice = banned, Rome = banned. Only possible during transit through rural areas.

### Apps & Websites
- **D-Flight platform:** https://www.d-flight.it/ (mandatory check before every flight)

### Practical Assessment: NOT RECOMMENDED for your Italy itinerary
Venice is 100% banned. Rome's historic center is 100% banned. The EUR 90 registration fee makes this extremely poor value given you cannot fly at any of your planned destinations. Italy has the harshest cost-to-opportunity ratio of all four countries.

| Country | Tourist drone flying practical? | Registration cost | Key itinerary locations flyable? | Overall verdict |

### Bottom Line

**For this specific 14-day itinerary, bringing a drone offers extremely poor return on the hassle involved.** Every major destination on your route (Paris, Versailles, Cologne, Berchtesgaden, Venice, Rome) is either a complete no-fly zone or heavily restricted. The only realistic flying opportunities are:

1. **Rural Germany** during the drive between Munich and Neuschwanstein — pull over in open farmland
2. **Rural Switzerland** if you find an alpine meadow away from Grindelwald village, helicopter routes, and protected areas

To fly legally in even one country, you would need:
- Country-specific registration (different system in each country)
- Third-party liability insurance valid in that country
- A1/A3 certificate completion
- Constant checking of official airspace maps

### If You Still Want to Bring the Drone

1. **Choose ONE country** to register in (Germany offers the best hassle-to-opportunity ratio at EUR 20)
2. **Get EU drone insurance** before departing China — a short-term policy covering all EU/EASA countries
3. **Use a sub-249g drone** (DJI Mini series) to minimize requirements
4. **Download the apps** before departure: DFS DrohnenApp (Germany), Geoportail (France), D-Flight (Italy)
5. **Only fly in verified rural areas** after checking the maps
6. **Never fly near ANY tourist landmark, city center, castle, national park, or heritage site**

### If You Decide NOT to Bring the Drone

This is the most practical recommendation for this itinerary. Instead:
- Use your phone/camera for photos and videos
- Consider a DJI Pocket gimbal camera for smooth handheld video — no regulations apply
- Many landmarks sell excellent aerial photos/postcards taken with permitted commercial drones
